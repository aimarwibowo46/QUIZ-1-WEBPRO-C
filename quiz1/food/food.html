These are local cuisine you need to try in Tegal.

Tahu Aci

Tahu Aci is an Indonesian food that has a savory and crunchy taste. Tofu Aci made from yellow tofu, has a rectangular shape with a side of about 7cm. The square tofu is split in half to form 2 right triangles. The part of the tofu that is split is given aci dough made of starch (aci), pieces of kocai leaves, garlic and several other spices.

Tahu Aci is a typical tofu from Tegal Regency, namely Slawi. Almost along the road in the Tegal area, there are many traders selling it. The traders usually sell using streetcarts and peddle from 4 pm until the evening.


Sate Kambing

Sate Tegal or Sate Kambing Tegal is satay from Tegal, Slawi, and surrounding areas made from mutton or young lamb, cut into cubes (±1.5 - 2 cm) arranged on a bamboo skewer combined with fat (gajih) and liver or kidney. Then the sate meat is grilled over wood charcoal coals or coconut shell charcoal until cooked. The aroma that comes from burning this satay has a very distinctive smell. How to burn does not need to be dipped in diluted sweet soy sauce but as is, aka plain. If you ask to be grilled plain in a satay stall in Yogyakarta, this method is close to the original method of grilling in Tegal, mutton which may not be the same as in Tegal. Sate Tegal is usually served with soy sauce seasoning consisting of sweet soy sauce, cayenne pepper, shallots and tomatoes. In Tegal, satay is sold in kodi units (= 20 skewers) and usually costs IDR 80,000 to IDR 90,000.

Sate Kambing Tegal in Jakarta is known as Sate Kambing Muda Tegal. In Tegal it is called Sate Kambing Balibul from the slopes of Mount Slamet, the area of ​​Mount Guci, to the foot of the mountains in the Slawi and Adiwerna (Benjaran) areas and also in the city of Tegal. Tegal itself became the first city to introduce satay with raw materials for young goats under five months old, or commonly called balibul.

Sate Tegal contains high cholesterol so that some people cannot enjoy it freely. Sate Tegal is usually eaten with fluffy white rice or rice cake with delicious goat curry. What distinguishes Tegal satay from other regional satays is the relatively larger piece of meat compared to satay from other regions. The meat is selected from young goat and lamb, resulting in a very tender satay.


Teh Poci

Tea pots are teas that are typically brewed in clay pots and cups. This drink is added with rock sugar and drunk hot. A drink that is very much liked by the people of Tegal, Slawi, Pemalang, Brebes, and surrounding areas.

There is a WASGITEL term for tea pot which stands for wangi, panas, sepet, legi, lan (and) kentel (thick), which means hot, sweet, fragrant tea with jasmine flowers and thick black / thick.

Poci tea usually uses green tea or jasmine tea which gives off a distinctive aroma, and is usually served in the morning, afternoon, or evening accompanied by snacks. The teapots used for brewing teapots are usually the inside of the teapots that are never washed, but just throw away the rest of the tea. It is believed by the people of Tegal that the remaining tea crust will add to the taste and aroma of the teapot, making it even more delicious.


Nasi Lengko

Sega Lengko (Nasi Lengko in Indonesian, Lengko rice in English) is a typical cirebonese dish in Cirebon, Indramayu, Brebes, Tegal and surrounding areas.

The ingredients are white rice (better hot), fried tempeh, fried tofu, cucumbers (fresh raw, chopped), bean sprouts (boiled), leaves of chives (cut into small pieces), fried onions, peanut sauce (such as salad seasoning) , spicy or not, depending on taste), and a thin soy sauce poured over the top.

Fried tempeh and tofu are cut into small pieces and placed on a plate of rice. Chopped cucumber is sprinkled on top. Well-cooked bean sprouts and peanut sauce are poured over it, with pieces of spring onion leaves. After this it is given a taste of soy sauce until browned, and sprinkled with fried onions. Aci crackers and a piece of white, round or square, to be complementary ingredients. The crackers smeared with ketchup is a popular appetizer.


Soto Tauco

For Soto fans, Tegal also offers Soto culinary but with a different taste typical of Tegal. With stuffed shredded chicken, sliced tripe, bean sprouts, fried brambang, what makes this Tegal cuisine different is the addition of Tauco in it. This makes the taste of Soto very distinctive with a combination of sweet, sour, salty, and the aroma of Tauco.
